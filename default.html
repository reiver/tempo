Tempo

the speed or pace at which a piece of music is played or a given activity occurs

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

Tempo is how fast or slow music (or some other activity) "moves". You can think of it as being like the speed of the music.

Beats per Minute

Tempo is typically measured in beats per minute (BPM).

For example, a tempo of 60 BPM means there is one beat every second. And, for example, a tempo of 120 BPM means there are two beats every second.

Songs with a higher BPM tend to feel faster and more energetic. Songs with a lower BPM tend to feel calmer and more relaxed.

A slow lullaby might have a slow tempo to help people relax. A dance song often has a fast tempo that makes people want to move. Changing the tempo can make the same music feel very different.

Tempo Change

In music, a tempo change is usually multiplicative.

For example, going from 60 BPM to 120 BPM to speed up — 120 = 60 × 2. Or, for example, going from 60 BPM to 30 BPM to slow down — 30 = 60 ÷ 2.

For most people, going from 60 BPM to 120 BPM, and going from 70 BPM to 140 BPM, feels like the same speed up — i.e., going twice as fast.

The ratio matters more from a perception point of view.
